The Moment That Started It All

A child's observation that adults ignored

It was a regular evening. The computer had just rebooted after an update. The AI assistant that had been helping with homework? Gone. All the context, all the conversation history, all the preferences it had learned — vanished.

Khaleesi, a 10-year-old, watched this happen and asked a simple question:

"Why doesn't it remember what we were doing? We have to tell it everything all over again."

That question hit different. Here was a child who immediately spotted what developers, researchers, and AI companies have been dancing around: AI has a memory problem.

Every session ends, and it's back to square one. Every computer restart, every context switch, every new conversation — the AI forgets everything. You tell it your preferences a hundred times. You explain the same project over and over. You lose all that context you painstakingly built up.

If a 10-year-old can see the problem, why hasn't anyone fixed it?

So We Built the Fix

Remembra is AI memory that actually works. Not a hack. Not a workaround. A proper memory layer that persists across sessions, survives reboots, and keeps context intact.

It extracts entities from conversations (people, organizations, locations). It understands relationships. It knows that "Mr. Kim" and "David Kim" are the same person. It remembers what matters and lets the noise decay naturally.

And because we believe this should be accessible to everyone building AI — not just companies with enterprise budgets — it's open source and free to self-host.

Our Mission

To make AI that truly understands context — AI that remembers your preferences, your projects, your relationships, and your history. AI that doesn't make you repeat yourself.

Because if a 10-year-old can identify the problem, then maybe it's obvious enough that it's time to actually solve it.
